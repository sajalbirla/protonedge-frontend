Tax Litigation Services

Tax litigation services in India refer to the professional assistance provided by legal and tax experts to individuals or businesses facing disputes or controversies with tax authorities. Tax litigation arises when there is a disagreement between taxpayers and tax authorities over tax liabilities, assessments, refunds, or other tax-related matters. These disputes may pertain to income tax, goods and services tax (GST), customs duty, excise duty, or other tax types.
Key aspects of tax litigation services in India include:
Representation and Advocacy
Tax litigation experts, including tax lawyers and chartered accountants, represent clients before various tax authorities, appellate tribunals, and courts. They present legal arguments and evidence to support their clients' positions and protect their rights.
Assessments and Appeals
Tax litigation services cover both the initial assessment stage and the subsequent appeals process. Tax professionals assist in responding to tax notices, filing objections, and preparing appeals against adverse tax assessments
Dispute Resolution
Tax consultants aim to resolve tax disputes through alternative dispute resolution mechanisms, such as settlement negotiations or mediation, to avoid prolonged litigation.
High Court & Supreme Court Representation
In case the dispute escalates, tax litigators can represent clients in High Courts and the Supreme Court of India, where complex tax matters are adjudicated.
Advisory Services
Tax litigation experts provide clients with legal advice and opinions on tax matters to help them make informed decisions and understand the potential tax implications of their actions.
International Tax Controversies
Support in cases involving international tax issues, including double taxation, tax treaties, and issues related to permanent establishment.
Stay of Demands
Requesting stay orders to halt tax authorities from taking coercive measures during the pendency of the tax dispute. Compliance and Risk Management: Advising clients on tax compliance practices to minimise the risk of future disputes and litigation.
